# Case Study: UPI and Online Payment Fraud

## What is the Crime?
UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud involves cybercriminals manipulating victims into authorizing unauthorized money transfers. While the UPI architecture is secure, scammers rely on "social engineering" to trick users into bypassing their own security.

## How it Typically Happens (Step-by-Step)
1. **The Bait:** A college student lists an item (like a used textbook or bicycle) for sale on an online marketplace or student WhatsApp group.
2. **The Hook:** The scammer immediately contacts the student, agrees to the price without bargaining, and insists on paying via a UPI app (like Google Pay or PhonePe).
3. **The Trap:** Instead of sending money, the scammer sends a "Collect Request" or a QR code to the student. 
4. **The Manipulation:** The scammer falsely instructs the student, "Scan this QR code and enter your UPI PIN to receive the payment into your account."
5. **The Consequence:** The student, believing they are accepting a payment, enters their secret UPI PIN. Instantly, the funds are deducted from the student's bank account and transferred to the scammer.

## Who is Targeted and the Consequences
College students are frequent targets because they regularly use peer-to-peer payment apps for splitting bills, paying for hostels, and buying/selling second-hand items. The consequences include immediate financial loss, severe emotional distress, and a tedious process of filing police reports, often with a low chance of recovering the stolen funds.
