# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

## ✅ 5 DO's
1. **Do review your privacy settings regularly:** Ensure your personal accounts are set to private and you know exactly who can see your posts and personal information.
2. **Do think before you post:** Remember that the internet is permanent. Even deleted posts, comments, or stories can be screenshotted and saved.
3. **Do build a professional presence:** Keep platforms like LinkedIn and GitHub updated with your current technical skills (C++, Python) and academic projects.
4. **Do verify information before sharing:** Check the credibility of a news source or forwarded message to avoid spreading misinformation or fake news.
5. **Do report and block:** Actively use platform tools to report cyberbullying, harassment, or suspicious spam accounts to keep your digital space safe.

## ❌ 5 DON'TS
1. **Don't overshare sensitive personal details:** Never post photos of your ID cards, flight tickets, home address, or phone number where anyone can see them.
2. **Don't engage with internet trolls:** Avoid getting into heated, unprofessional arguments in comment sections, as this leaves a negative digital footprint.
3. **Don't post inappropriate or illegal content:** Things that seem like a joke now can be easily found by future employers or internship coordinators and cost you a job.
4. **Don't plagiarize:** Do not copy someone else's artwork, code, or writing and post it as your own without giving proper credit to the original creator.
5. **Don't accept requests from complete strangers:** Be cautious about who you let into your digital network, especially if the account looks like a bot or has zero mutual connections.
